Page 6 = Stress Management

Stress is the normal reaction the body has when trying to adapt when changes occur resulting in physical, emotional, mental and intellectual responses. Effective stress management helps you break hold of the reaction and can make you happier, healthier and more productive.

Signs of stress are:

  • Muscle tension or headaches
  • Lack of sleep
  • Irritable or mood changes
  • Shortage of motivation
  • Concentration issues
  • Overwhelmed or anxious when doing particular tasks

These steps can be taken to manage stress:

1. Monitor stress.

a. Identify the triggers or what makes you stressed.

b. Notice the warning signs that are the cause of your stress.

c. Make sure that when these triggers/signs are sensed take the following steps.

2. Change the stressor.

a. Take the activity that makes you stressed out of the equation.

b. Stop putting that unnecessary pressure on yourself.

3. Exercise.

a. Regular exercise can relieve tension, relax the mind and reduce anxiety.

4. Spend time with loved ones.

a. Being with people you love can motivate you, which can resolve personal conflicts.

b. Talk to your loved ones, inform them of your issue. This can help for them to understand your issues and help you in any way possible.

5. Look after your health.

a. Manage a healthy diet, ensure you get enough sleep, and avoid the usage of drugs and alcohol to cope with stress.

6. Relax.

a. Take some time off.

b. Do breathing exercise or things that you love.

c. This will help take your mind of the issues and can potentially solve the problem.
