Page 5 = Mental Health

Mental health is a broad overview which contains our emotional, social and psychological wellbeing. These particular things can affect how we think, feel and act on a daily basis. It determines how we manage stress, communicate with others and make important choices. Mental health is vital and is just as important as physical health. Improving your mental health will help you develop as an individual, both physically, mentally and emotionally.

These are some steps taken to develop and improve your mental health:

1. Seek out to someone your trust.

a. Talk about your feelings.

b. Talk about what troubles you.

c. Let your emotions and thoughts out.

2. Keep active.

a. Mental health correlates with physical health.

b. Increasing physical activity can enhance your concentration and self-esteem.

3. Eat healthy.

a. Make sure that you are nutrient rich foods.

b. Do not eat fast-food, and other junk food regularly.

c. These things can promote a healthy mindset which will enrich your mental health.

4. Surround yourself around good people.

a. Cut out time wasters.

b. Surround yourself around people with the same interests.

c. Make new friends.

5. Take a break.

a. Relax and let everything around you fade away.

b. Go somewhere you love, and have some fun.

6. Do something your good at.

7. Accept who you are.

a. Everyone is different, that is just how life is, you cannot do anything about that.

b. Change the things you can change leave everything else.

8. Ask for help.

a. Everyone has problems asking for help is a great way to fix them.

b. Do not be afraid to ask for help, no one is judging.

c. Beyond Blue helpline: 1300 22 4636.

When stress become overwhelming and prolong, the risks of mental health problems and medical problems increase. Long -term stress can increase mental health problems such as anxiety and depressions, substance use problems, sleep problems, pain and bodily complaints such as muscle tensions. To overcome this below is an overview of stress and stress management tips.

Stress and managing it

Stress is the normal reaction the body has when trying to adapt when changes occur resulting in physical, emotional, mental and intellectual responses. Effective stress management helps you break hold of the reaction and can make you happier, healthier and more productive.

Signs of stress are:

  • Muscle tension or headaches
  • Lack of sleep
  • Irritable or mood changes
  • Shortage of motivation
  • Concentration issues
  • Overwhelmed or anxious when doing particular tasks

These steps can be taken to manage stress:

1. Monitor stress.

a. Identify the triggers or what makes you stressed.

b. Notice the warning signs that are the cause of your stress.

c. Make sure that when these triggers/signs are sensed take the following steps.

2. Change the stressor.

a. Take the activity that makes you stressed out of the equation.

b. Stop putting that unnecessary pressure on yourself.

3. Exercise.

a. Regular exercise can relieve tension, relax the mind and reduce anxiety.

4. Spend time with loved ones.

a. Being with people you love can motivate you, which can resolve personal conflicts.

b. Talk to your loved ones, inform them of your issue. This can help for them to understand your issues and help you in any way possible.

5. Look after your health.

a. Manage a healthy diet, ensure you get enough sleep, and avoid the usage of drugs and alcohol to cope with stress.

6. Relax.

a. Take some time off.

b. Do breathing exercise or things that you love.

c. This will help take your mind of the issues and can potentially solve the problem.
